Invite integrity

Flagged inviters

A pending invite only counts after a real-visitor signal (a chosen username plus a real session) and expires on its own after a week, so a drive-by invite-link bot earns nothing. Listed here are inviters whose pending invites still look automated. Investigate to see the pattern, then remove a flagged cluster. Active invites are never touched, every removal is logged, and a developer can undo it, so a clean inviter is never hurt by a mistake.

Guide

Welcome to the moderation dashboard. Everything here is proven by your staff key and checked on the server, so nothing on this page can be faked from a browser. The dashboard keeps the team accountable to each other: every action is logged, visible to all staff, and permanent.

Your role

What each level can do. The chain is enforced on the server: no one can act on someone at their level or above.

Junior mod (L1)
  • Kick, warn, and force-rename in a room
  • Report and trace users
  • No ban, IP block, room lock, slow mode, or board clear
Full mod (L2)
  • Every junior power, plus ban and IP block (up to 7 days)
  • Close or lock rooms, slow mode, wipe text, clear board
  • Review reports and applications; may grant junior keys
Developer
  • Every mod power on anyone except other developers
  • Permanent IP blocks, sees raw IPs, manages mod keys
  • Feature flags, room size, maintenance, spectate, and more
The tabs

Activity

A live feed of every staff action and every identity event (sign-ins and name changes). Each card is one event, color coded by category. Search by name, action, IP, room, or message, and filter the feed by type.

Reports

Users flagged by the community, grouped by person and most reported first. The card header is the reported user; the list below is who reported them and why. Act right from the card: kick, or IP block for a set time. A reported user who goes offline can still be blocked using their last known address.

Applications

Active members applying for the junior role. Approve to mint and deliver their key automatically, or decline. Applications are never auto-accepted; a human always reviews.

Ban list DEV

Every active IP block with a live countdown, who placed it, and the reason. One tap unbans. A permanent block is tagged in red.

Moderators DEV

Every active mod key. Grant a new junior or full key (shown once), promote or demote a level, or revoke instantly with a live downgrade.

Sessions DEV

Who is connected on each staff key right now, plus every IP a key has ever connected from. A key live on more than one IP, or a brand-new IP, may mean it was shared or leaked.

Good to know

Trace a user

Click any underlined name in the feed to focus that person. You will see every name they have used and every action taken against them, so a troublemaker can be followed even after a rename.

Leave a note

Type under any activity entry to add a permanent comment or ask why an action was taken. All staff see comments live, so use them to keep each other in the loop.

IP privacy

Raw IP addresses are shown to developers only. Moderators can still ban or IP block a user, because the server handles the address for them, but never see it.

Staff colors

Developer names are red and moderator names are orange throughout the dashboard, so you can tell at a glance who took an action.

Activity categories

The colored left edge and icon on each Activity card tell you what kind of event it is.
